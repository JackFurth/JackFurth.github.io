Intro

Jack Furth was born in 1998, and dealt with this fact until his untimely death. Convinced by a broad, systemic power structure that he was better off pursuing a higher education, Furth found himself enrolled in Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University in the Fall of 2017. He quickly purchased a bucket from the one-dollar-store to use as a makeshift waste basket and started a presumably lucrative career as an English major in the Liberal Arts program, were it not cut short by the cold grip of death. Two years after joining the institution, Furth requested a position in an 11AM Writing and Digital Media Course, where he was asked to make a website that included links to other assignments he had been asked to complete over the semester. This is that same website, now run by a small administration staff of Furthean admirers. Jack Furth passed away moments after first publishing the site, having caught a particularily bad strain of the common cold.

Work

Furth focused primarily on four projects during his time in Andy Lautenschlager's Writing and Digital Media course, all of which were acclaimed, won various awards, and essenitally changed the course of the human race. We are proud to present you this catalouge of work in its entirety. Furth began his self-exploration in the written form, tackling pressing environmental issues of his day. His first piece, Big Lawnmower Has Brainwashed You into Wanting an Ugly Lawn, is most well-known as starting the Anti-Lawn social movement, which would eventually become the modern neo-Democratic party in America. His follow-up, Install a Bidet, You Coward, is less well-known--though it is considered by scholars to include some of his most subversive rhetorical tactics. Collaborating with Michelle and Chris (their last names are lost to time), Furth developed a podcast on the local music scene of Blacksburg, Virginia, now a sovereign state. The trio also composed a documentary on Furth's artistic ventures outside the classoom, from which most of the details of his life are known to us.
